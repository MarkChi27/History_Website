October 13, 1970

The Government of Canada, under Liberal Prime Minister Pierre Elliot Trudeau, recognizes the People’s Republic of China as the sole legitimate government of China. This independent gesture, which will contribute to breaking the isolation of Mao Zedong’s Communist China on the world stage, marks the start of diplomatic relations between Canada and China.

Prime Minister Trudeau will travel to China in 1973 to meet Mao Zedong, becoming the first Canadian head of government to make an official visit to China.
