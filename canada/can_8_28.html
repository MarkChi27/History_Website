August 28, 1968

Les belles-sœurs, a play written in 1965 by Québécois dramatist Michel Tremblay, is staged for the first time at Montréal’s Théâtre du Rideau Vert.

The two-act play had first been given a public reading on March 4, 1968 at the Centre du Théâtre d’Aujourd’hui.

Initially deemed controversial, Tremblay’s dramatic comedy, directed by André Brassard, will later be considered a landmark play in Quebec’s dramatic arts, being one of the first to use joual (slang) and to delve into the daily lives of Montréal’s working-class women in the 1960s.

Translated into more than 30 languages and staged in a number of countries, Michel Tremblay’s famous play will be adapted into a musical.
