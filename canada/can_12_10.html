December 10, 1948

At a meeting in Paris, the General Assembly of the United Nations (UN) adopts the Universal Declaration of Human Rights. The first draft of the declaration was written by Canadian lawyer John Peters Humphrey. In the vote by the 58 member states of the General Assembly, 8 abstained and none dissented.

In early 1947, the Commission on Human Rights authorized a preliminary draft International Bill of Human Rights. A committee of different member states selected with regard for geographical distribution was later tasked with writing a document setting out universal human rights.
