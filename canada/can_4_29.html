April 29, 1903

The town of Frank, Northwest Territories (now in Alberta), is buried under 74 million tons of rock that crash down from Turtle Mountain.

Local Blackfoot tribes avoided establishing a settlement near the base of the mountain that they call the “mountain that moves.”

Seventy people lose their lives in the disaster. The 23 survivors include miners who were underground and dug themselves out, and 4 girls: Lillian Clarke (15), Fernie Watkins, Gladys Ennis (27 months) and Marion Leitch (15 months).
