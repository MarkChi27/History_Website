May 11, 2000

The Nisga’a Final Agreement comes into effect. The agreement was signed in April 1999 by the governments of Canada, British Columbia and the Nisga’a Nation. The agreement enshrines land and water rights, as well as self-governance and control over forestry and fishing resources within their territory, for the Nisga’a Nation.

The treaty, or comprehensive agreement, is the first modern treaty to be settled in British Columbia. Previous treaties were the 1854 Douglas Treaties for Vancouver and the 1899 Treaty 8 for Northern B.C.
