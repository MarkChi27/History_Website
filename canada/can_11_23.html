Fredericton City Hall in New Brunswick is designated a National Historic Site of Canada. The building was constructed in 1875â€“1876, making it the oldest city hall in the Atlantic region still used for municipal government.

The building at 397 Queen Street in Fredericton previously included some uses beyond municipal matters. An opera hall was located on the second floor, and until 1951, there was a market in the basement.

Fredericton City Hall features cubic architecture. The building includes a red brick facade and a central tower. The tower clock is 2.4 metres in diameter and retains its original brass mechanism.
