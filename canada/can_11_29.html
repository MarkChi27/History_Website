November 29, 1963

Trans-Canada Airlines Flight 831 crashes in Sainte-Thérèse-de-Blainville, near Montréal, killing 118, including 111 passengers.

The DC-8F took off from Montréal’s Dorval international airport at 6:28 p.m., headed for the international airport in Toronto. The last radio contact authorized the plane to make a left turn toward Saint-Eustache. The aircraft crashes in a swampy field about 8 minutes after takeoff, leaving no survivors.
