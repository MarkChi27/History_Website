July 31, 1868

The Rupert's Land Act, 1868 is passed by the British Parliament and Queen Victoria. It officially sanctions the return of all rights and claims to the territory and resources of Rupert's Land from the Hudson's Bay Company.

The Act cedes the land of the Hudson Bay basin to Canada. The vast geographical area covered by this land transfer extends today from northern Quebec and Ontario, across all of Manitoba, and into most of Saskatchewan and part of Alberta.

In 1869, Sir John A. Macdonald will begin arrangements for the purchase of Rupert's Land and the North-Western Territory. They will be officially added to Canada as the Northwest Territories in 1870.
