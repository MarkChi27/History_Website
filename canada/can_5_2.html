May 2, 1964

Northern Dancer wins the Kentucky Derby, the first Canadian horse to do so. He will get a hero’s welcome when he returns home to Toronto – the mayor will even give him the key to the city!

The racehorse will be named 1964’s Athlete of the Year by Canadian sportswriters. Over the course of his two-year career, Northern Dancer will win 14 of the 18 races he competes in. Considered a Canadian icon, he will be the first horse inducted into the Canadian Sports Hall of Fame, in 1965.
