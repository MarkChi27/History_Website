December 28, 1929

Legendary hockey goaltender Terrence (“Terry”) Gordon Sawchuk is born in Winnipeg, Manitoba.

Terry Sawchuk will begin playing organized hockey at the age of 10. At 16, Terry will move to Galt (now Cambridge), Ontario, to join the junior Red Wings.

Terry Sawchuk will work his way through the Red Wings organization. He will make his debut in the National Hockey League in January 1950. In 1951, he will receive the Calder Trophy as rookie of the year. During his career, he will also win the Vézina Trophy, for fewest goals allowed, three times. He will record an amazing total of 103 career shutouts.

Terry Sawchuk will win three Stanley Cups with the Detroit Red Wings, in 1952, 1954 and 1955, and one with the Toronto Maple Leafs, in 1967.

Terry Sawchuk will die accidently in 1970 after scuffling with teammate Ron Stewart. Sawchuk will be inducted into the Hockey Hall of Fame in 1971.
