August 31, 1896

Félix-Antoine Savard is born in the city of Québec.

Ordained as a priest in 1922, he will find himself drawn to writing. Savard’s first novel, Menaud maître-draveur, will be published in 1937 and enjoy great success. Inspired by real-life events and evoking olden days in the Charlevoix region, the book will immediately be met with resounding critical acclaim and later become deeply ingrained in Quebec’s cultural landscape.

Encouraged by the success of his first novel, Savard will leave the priesthood to become a professor of literature at Université Laval in 1940. He will revise his poetic novel five times (in 1938, 1944, 1957, 1960 and 1964), before settling on a final version in 1967. After this 30-year maturation process, the book will be a fine example of a patiently crafted, successful literary work. Menaud maître-draveur will be translated into English in 1947 (Boss of the River), and then again in 1976 (Master of the River).
