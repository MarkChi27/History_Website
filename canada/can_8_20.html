August 20, 1970

Two F3-force tornadoes leave six dead and some 200 injured in northern Ontario.

Sudbury takes a direct hit from the first tornado. In 10 minutes, powerful winds gusting up to 320 km/h destroy dozens of homes and buildings over a 13-kilometre area. Many pumping stations also sustain serious damage. The tornado’s path of destruction tears through several Sudbury neighbourhoods, including Copper Cliff, Lively and Robinson. Five people die and 150 suffer injuries.

The storm continues on its path and spawns a second F3-force tornado that touches down an hour later in Field, 50 km east of Sudbury. This tornado kills one person and injuring 50. A factory and the local church are destroyed.

The two tornadoes rank among the 10 deadliest in Canada. They cause $17 million in damage, or 100 million in today’s dollars.
