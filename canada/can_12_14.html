December 14, 1943

Captain Paul Triquet of the Royal 22nd Regiment is awarded the Victoria Cross (VC) for valour in the face of the enemy during the Italian Campaign. He leads soldiers to victory at Casa Berardi.

Triquet is the second Canadian VC recipient during the Second World War and the first to receive the honour in the Italian Campaign. Born in Cabano, Quebec, and the only French Canadian to receive the VC for actions in Italy, Triquet will eventually reach the rank of Brigadier-General.
