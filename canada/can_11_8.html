November 8, 1946

Viola Desmond refuses to leave a whites-only area in a movie theatre. For her resistance against racism, she will be jailed, convicted and fined.

The court case of this businesswoman from Nova Scotia will be the first known legal challenge against racial segregation by a Black woman in Canada. She will be pardoned posthumously by the Government of Nova Scotia in 2010, 63 years after her arrest.

This icon of human rights and freedom in Canada will be the first woman on the new $10 banknote in 2018.
