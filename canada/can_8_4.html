August 4, 1914

The British Empire (including Canada) declares war on Germany. Almost six weeks after the assassination of Archduke Franz Ferdinand, the Great War is under way in Europe because of a domino effect of political and historical alliances.

Canada is embroiled in the conflict as soon as the United Kingdom declares war. Over 650,000 Canadians will be involved in the First World War, serving on the home front, in the trenches, at sea and in the air. Over 66,000 will die and another 172,000 will be injured. The war will end with an armistice on November 11, 1918, and the state of war will be revoked on June 28, 1919, with the Treaty of Versailles.
