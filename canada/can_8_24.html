August 24, 2001

Air Transat pilot Robert Piché heroically glides his stricken aircraft to safety in the Azores. A mechanical failure caused the plane’s engines to stop. All 293 passengers and 13 crew of Flight 236 survive.

The event is similar to the 1983 “Gimli Glider” incident in Manitoba, when pilot Robert Pearson safely landed an Air Canada 767 that ran out of fuel mid-flight.
