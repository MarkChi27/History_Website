October 11, 1942

Henry Larsen arrives in Halifax, Nova Scotia, aboard the St. Roch, a Royal Canadian Mounted Police schooner. After a 28-month voyage exploring Canadaâ€™s Arctic, the St. Roch is the second ship to navigate the Northwest Passage, and the first to do so from west to east. After setting sail on June 23, 1940, from Vancouver, British Columbia, the small wooden schooner was trapped in ice for much of its journey.

The St. Roch will be equipped with better engines after arriving in Halifax harbour.

In 1944, the schooner will complete the return trip to Vancouver in just 86 days, becoming the first ship to navigate the Northwest Passage in both directions.
