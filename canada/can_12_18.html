December 18, 1950

The 2nd Battalion, Princess Patriciaâ€™s Canadian Light Infantry arrives in Pusan, South Korea. The battalion, based in Shilo, Manitoba, left for Korea some three weeks earlier. This is the first Canadian battalion to arrive in the country during the Korean War, which started on June 25, 1950.

In mid-February 1951, Canada, Great Britain, Australia, New Zealand and India will form a Commonwealth Force. Canadian and other Commonwealth troops will participate in the Battle of Kapyong in April 1951. The Canadian battalion will help American soldiers to retreat safely and be awarded a U.S. Presidential Unit Citation for its actions in the battle.

Some 26,000 Canadians will serve in the conflict. A truce will be signed on July 27, 1953.
