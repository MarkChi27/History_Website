November 24, 1967

The Confederation Train rolls into Lachine station in Quebec. It will remain there until November 27. The train began its cross-country journey on January 9 in Victoria, British Columbia.

The Confederation Train and the Centenary Caravans are two initiatives to celebrate the 100th anniversary of Canada’s Confederation.

The train was Inaugurated on New Year’s Day 1967 by Pauline Vanier, wife of Governor General Georges Vanier. By the end of its journey, the Confederation Train will travel to every province in Canada, as well as Yukon and the Northwest Territories.

A number of exhibitions and shows are presented in the coach cars. The engine has the Centenary logo painted on it, and train’s horn plays the first four notes of “O Canada.”

The train will arrive in Nova Scotia in October, and its final destination will be Windsor station in Montréal, from November 30 to December 5.
