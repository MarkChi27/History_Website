May 31, 1997

The Confederation Bridge opens for interprovincial traffic between Prince Edward Island and New Brunswick. The 12.9-kilometre-long bridge spans the Northumberland Strait, linking the island, at Borden-Carleton, to the mainland, at Cape Jourimain National Wildlife Area.

A remarkable feat of engineering, the bridge is the world’s longest across “ice-covered waters.” Approximately 4,000 vehicles cross the bridge daily.
