December 13, 1979

The Progressive Conservative minority government falls after the New Democratic Party’s non-confidence motion on the budget passes. With Liberal Party support, the vote in the House of Commons is 139 to 133.

An austerity budget had been introduced by Finance Minister John Crosby on December 11, 1979.

Joe Clark will resign as prime minister, and an election will be called.

In the election on February 18, 1980, Clark will lose to Pierre Elliott Trudeau’s Liberals, who will form a majority government.
