November 28, 1956

The Government of Canada releases a million dollars of funds to take in Hungarian refugees fleeing their country in the aftermath of repression by the Union of Soviet Socialist Republics. Canada adopts a policy whereby the Department of Citizenship and Immigration pays the travel costs of these refugees.

In December, some 100 Hungarian refugees will arrive every day in Toronto. Although the immigration program will end in September 1958, the influx of immigrants will continue until 1959. In all, 37,565 Hungarian refugees will be taken in by Canada.
