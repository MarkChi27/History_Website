A massive overnight landslide devastates the town of St.-Jean-Vianney, in Quebecâ€™s Saguenay-Lac-Saint-Jean region. In a matter of moments, 31 people lose their lives and some 40 homes are swept away. The landslide leaves behind a massive crater 600 metres wide and 30 metres deep, larger than any previously recorded.

The village is declared unsafe for habitation because of the Leda clay, and the residents are relocated. The site is still abandoned today, and the 324,000-square-metre crater can be seen on Google Earth.
