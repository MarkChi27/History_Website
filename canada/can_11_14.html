November 14, 1938

The Lions Gate Bridge officially opens to traffic in Vancouver, British Columbia.

Spanning more than 470 metres (1,520 metres including the approach spans), the suspension bridge connects Stanley Park, near downtown Vancouver, to West Vancouver, on the north shore.

An iconic symbol of Vancouver, the Lions Gate Bridge will be officially inaugurated by King George VI and Queen Elizabeth on May 29, 1939. It will be recognized as a National Historic Site of Canada in 2004. The design is similar to that of the Golden Gate Bridge in San Francisco.
