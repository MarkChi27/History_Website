August 12, 1994

A Major League Baseball strike starts, during a dream season for the Montreal Expos. They have the best record in the majors (74 wins and 40 losses).

The labour dispute will last 232 days and have a devastating effect on the team. To make up for huge financial losses, the organization will not be able to keep many of its best players the next year.

The Montreal Expos will never have another season like 1994. The team will move to Washington, D.C., after the 2004 season.
