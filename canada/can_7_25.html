July 25, 1990

Following the failure of the Meech Lake Accord, which would have amended the Constitution of Canada, independent MP and former minister Lucien Bouchard joins other MPs who left their parties to create the Bloc Québécois.

Initially an informal coalition of MPs to defend the interests of Quebec and promote sovereignty, the Bloc Québécois will become an official party at its founding convention on June 15, 1991.
