November 17, 1903

Inspector Francis Joseph Fitzgerald of the North West Mounted Police builds a police detachment in the High Arctic, on Herschel Island in the Beaufort Sea.

The island is part of the Yukon Territory, despite the presence of American whalers. It will be Fitzgerald’s base for six years, during which he will stop the whisky trade and collect customs duties, asserting Canadian sovereignty under difficult conditions.

The Nova Scotia veteran of the Boer War will die with three colleagues on their way to Dawson City in 1911, a trip that will become known as “The Lost Patrol.”
