September 1, 1905

Saskatchewan and Alberta join Confederation together as the eighth and ninth Canadian provinces.

By creating these provinces, the Canadian government of Wilfrid Laurier extends and occupies the land to the west of the Great Lakes, to prevent American expansionism.

During negotiations in 1902 and 1905, the plan was to have one large province. But things turned out differently, as Laurier opted for two provinces because they would be easier to administer.
