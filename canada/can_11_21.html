November 21, 1988

Ethel Blondin-Andrew is the first First Nations woman elected to the House of Commons.

Ethel Blondin-Andrew earned a Bachelor of Education at the University of Alberta in 1974 and taught in several schools in northern Canada. She begins her political career in 1988. Elected as a Liberal MP for the riding of Western Arctic in the Northwest Territories, she will serve in the House of Commons for five terms, until 2006.
