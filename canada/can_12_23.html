December 23, 1900

Canadian inventor Reginald Aubrey Fessenden conducts the first wireless transmission of the human voice.

While working for the Weather Bureau in the United States, Fessenden carries out an experiment at the meteorological station on Cobb Island in Maryland. Fessenden speaks these words into his radio microphone: “Is it snowing where you are, Mr. Thiessen?” His assistant in Virginia replies by telegraph that it is indeed snowing there.

In 1902, Fessenden will found a company, the National Electric Signaling Company, to continue his work on electromagnetic waves. He will be designated a Person of National Historical Significance in 1943.
