July 29, 1916

In Matheson, Ontario, windstorms whip small scattered forest fires into a raging 200,000-hectare inferno. The Northern Ontario towns of Matheson, Iroquois Falls, Cochrane, Porquis Junction, Kelso, Nushka and Ramore are devastated in the blaze.

An estimated 223 people will die in the blaze, making it the deadliest forest fire in Canadian history. The fire will be a catalyst for federal and provincial regulations to prevent and fight forest fires.
