August 7, 1950

Prime Minister Louis St-Laurent creates the Canadian Army Special Force to fight in the Korean War. The three-year conflict broke out on June 25, 1950, when North Korea invaded South Korea.

Canada had already committed the destroyers HMCS Athabaskan, HMCS Cayuga and HMCS Sioux, as well as RCAF Squadron 426. In total, 26,791 Canadian military personnel will serve in Korea between 1950 and 1957, when the last Canadian soldiers will return home.
