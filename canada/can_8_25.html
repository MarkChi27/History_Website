August 25, 1873

An unusually violent hurricane destroys everything in its path in Nova Scotia, leaving nearly 500 people, mostly sailors, dead. A hundred more people are also killed in Newfoundland. Property damage is extensive too: 900 homes and 1,200 boats are destroyed.

The event is similar to the 1983 “Gimli Glider” incident in Manitoba, when pilot Robert Pearson safely landed an Air Canada 767 that ran out of fuel mid-flight.
