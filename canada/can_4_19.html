April 19, 1915

Montréal native Édouard Fabre wins the Boston Marathon. Fabre’s long and successful career will see him run in 315 races and collect more than 150 trophies. In 1964, he will be inducted into the Canada's Sports Hall of Fame.

Other notable Canadian runners who won the Boston Marathon include Thomas Longboat from the Six Nations reserve near Brantford, Ontario (1907), Gérard Côté (1940) and Jacqueline Gareau of L'Annonciation, Quebec (1980). These runners set new time records in their eras.
