July 15, 1870

Manitoba joins Confederation and becomes the fifth Canadian province. Before Confederation, the territory was controlled by the Hudson's Bay Company, and the earliest settlements included Red River (within the Selkirk Concession) and Batoche.

After it enters Confederation, the population of Manitoba will surge. Winnipeg's population will grow exponentially in one year, from 1,000 to 9,000 inhabitants.
