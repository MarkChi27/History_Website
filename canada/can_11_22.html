November 22, 1994

Roméo Adrien LeBlanc is appointed as Governor General of Canada by Prime Minister Jean Chrétien. LeBlanc is the first Acadian to be named to the position.

Born in L’Anse-aux-Cormiers, New Brunswick, Roméo LeBlanc joined the CBC in 1960 as a correspondent in Ottawa, the United Kingdom and the United States. In 1967, he became Prime Minister Lester B. Pearson’s press secretary. He remained in that role in Pierre Elliott Trudeau’s government until 1971.

LeBlanc launched his own political career in 1972, as the Liberal MP for the riding of Westmorland-Kent in New Brunswick. He served as Minister of Fisheries and the Environment, and was re-elected in 1979 and 1980. In 1984, he was appointed to the Senate and became Speaker in 1993.

Roméo Adrien LeBlanc will serve as Governor General from 1995 to 1999. He will die in 2009 at the age of 82.
