April 9, 1917

The Canadian Army begins one of the most important battles in its history: the capture of Vimy Ridge in France. Four Canadian divisions, a total of 15,000 infantrymen from all across Canada, storm the German positions. The Canadians will emerge victorious, capturing the crucial top of the ridge. The fighting will last for three days, with a total of 3,598 Canadians killed and 7,000 wounded.
