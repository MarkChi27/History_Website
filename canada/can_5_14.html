May 14, 1969

Homosexuality is decriminalized in Canada, with the passage of the Criminal Law Amendment Act (1968-1969). This omnibus bill was first introduced in December 1967 by Pierre Elliott Trudeau, Minister of Justice. The bill went through two revisions, C-195 and C-150, before finally passing third reading with a vote of 149 to 55.

This massive amendment to the Criminal Code of Canada included amendments to the law on abortion, lotteries and gun possession, and added new offences like impaired driving and cruelty to animals.
