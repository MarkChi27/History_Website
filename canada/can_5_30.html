May 30, 2005

The Canadian Red Cross is fined $30,000 after pleading guilty to distribution of contaminated blood. Between 1980 and 1990, an estimated 2,000 people were infected with HIV/AIDS, and another 30,000 people contracted Hepatitis C.

The Canadian Red Cross will no longer be involved in blood banks. It will be replaced by Canadian Blood Services and Héma-Québec. A class-action lawsuit will result in a $1-billion trust fund for victim compensation.
