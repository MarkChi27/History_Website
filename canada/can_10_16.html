October 16, 1970

During the October Crisis triggered by the Front de libération du Québec (FLQ), Pierre Elliott Trudeau’s federal government invokes the War Measures Act. This leads to the declaration of a state of emergency in Quebec. The FLQ is banned, civil liberties are suspended, and arrests and detentions without charge are authorized.

In the next few hours, 8,000 Canadian soldiers are deployed on the streets of Montréal, and nearly 500 people arrested. The following day, Pierre Laporte, Quebec’s Minister of Immigration, Manpower and Labour, will be found dead in the trunk of a car. The FLQ members who kidnapped him will be arrested and brought to justice.
