December 20, 1991

The Board of Governors of the National Hockey League (NHL) approves an unconditional, or permanent, franchise for the Ottawa Senators hockey team. After nearly 60 years, the team will be back in the capital.

The Ottawa Hockey Club was formed in 1883. The Senators joined the NHL in 1917, and left the city in 1934. After a difficult three-year campaign, Bruce Firestone and his real estate company succeed in bringing back the team. On December 6, 1990, Firestone was granted a conditional franchise by the NHL Board of Governors in Miami, Florida.
