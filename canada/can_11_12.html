November 12, 1939

Dr. Norman Bethune dies of septicemia (blood poisoning) after cutting his finger in surgery while operating a battlefield hospital during the Chinese Revolution.

This medical pioneer will be recognized internationally for developing a number of surgical treatments for tuberculosis as well as procedures for battlefront blood transfusion. He will also be noted for strengthening the bond between Canada and China, fostering our country’s reputation as a nation with a social conscience, and advocating for Canada’s universal health care system.
