October 29, 1929

Share prices in the Montreal Stock Exchange and Toronto Stock Exchange plummet in their worst drop ever, as the New York market crashes.

The Calgary Stock Exchange closes for a few hours on Black Tuesday, but reopens when traders think the situation is only temporary. They are wrong.

The crash spreads quickly around the globe. Governments quickly impose tariffs to protect their industries from dumping by other countries. However, this action causes a collapse in world trade, which ushers in the Great Depression.
