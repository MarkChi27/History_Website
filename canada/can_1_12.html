January 12, 1998

Louise Fréchette becomes Deputy Secretary-General of the United Nations. She is the first person in the position, newly created by Secretary-General Kofi Annan. In 2005, Forbes Magazine will rank her 65th among the 100 most powerful women in the world.

Louise Fréchette will serve as UN Deputy Secretary-General from 1998 to 2006. Before that, she worked in the federal public service and the Canadian diplomatic corps.
