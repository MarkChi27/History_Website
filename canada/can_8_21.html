August 21, 1952

British director Alfred Hitchcock arrives in the city of Québec to begin filming I Confess. The film stars, among others, Anne Baxter and Montgomery Clift. Several Quebec actors are also in the production, including Gilles Pelletier, Ovila Légaré and two young girls, Renée Hudon and Carmen Gingras.

The film tells the story of a priest who is unjustly accused of murder. He cannot reveal the actual murderer because of the confidentiality of the confessional. Hitchcock’s movie will be censored by the Catholic church at its world double premiere at Québec’s Cartier and Capitol cinemas on February 12, 1953.

Alfred Hitchcock will be so angered by this censorship that he will later vow never to return to the city.
