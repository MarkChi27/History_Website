December 29, 1921

William Lyon Mackenzie King is appointed Prime Minister of Canada, the 10th person to hold the office. The Liberal Party, which he leads, won 118 of the 235 seats in the House of Commons.

W.L.M. King will be Prime Minister of Canada for a total of 22 years. He will serve from 1921 to 1926, from 1926 to 1930, and from 1935 to 1948. King will be in office during some difficult times for the country, including the second half of the Great Depression in 1930 and all of the Second World War. He will be successful in bringing about autonomy for Canada, preserving national unity and introducing social reforms.
