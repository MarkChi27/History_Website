August 9, 1948

Paul-Émile Borduas and 15 young artists from the Automatists group sign and publish the Refus global artistic manifesto.

The hard-hitting declaration denounces the obscurantism of French-Canadian society at the time.

Through this manifesto, the Automatist artists, led by painters Paul-Émile Borduas and Jean-Paul Riopelle, seek to free their society from the ever-present religious morality of the Duplessis era.

Many view the publication of the Refus global as the basis for the Quiet Revolution and modern Quebec.
