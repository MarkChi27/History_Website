August 8, 1918

Canadian and Australian troops advance 13 kilometres into territory held by Germans in the Battle of Amiens. The battle will last four days and involve over 600 tanks, 2,000 aircraft and scores of heavy guns.

German morale will be hit hard, and the Canadian and Australian troops will be buoyed by their remarkable advance and victory against the enemy. However, Canadian casualties will be high, with over 11,800 dead and injured, including nearly 4,000 on the first day.

This “black day” for German morale, the first of the “Last Hundred Days,” will boost Allied troops and mark the beginning of the end of the First World War.
