December 9, 1956

A four-engine North Star plane operated by Trans-Canada Airlines crashes on Mount Slesse near Chilliwack, British Columbia, killing 59 passengers and 3 crew members. The plane left Vancouver at 6:10 p.m. for Calgary, Alberta. The last radio contact was at 7:10 p.m.

Among the victims were five players from the Saskatchewan Roughriders and the Winnipeg Blue Bombers, who were heading home after a Canadian Football League all-star game.

Ice and turbulence will be determined to have caused the crash.
