December 24, 2013

Frédéric Back passes away at the age of 89. The great illustrator, artist and animated film director will be remembered as a great Canadian artist and an environmental activist.

Frédéric Back was born in 1924. He lived and studied in Strasbourg, France, then learned art in Paris from Mathurin Méheut. He showed his first paintings at the Salon de la Marine in Paris in 1946 and 1947.

In 1948, Back established himself in Montréal. He taught at the École du Meuble and the Montreal School of Fine Arts. Back began working in the Radio-Canada/CBC graphic arts department in 1952. In 1968, he joined the new animation department at Radio-Canada/CBC. His animated films, enjoyed around the world, were nominated four times for Oscars. Back won twice: for Crac! (1981) and The Man Who Planted Trees / L’homme qui plantait des arbres (1987).
