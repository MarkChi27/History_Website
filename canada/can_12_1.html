December 1, 1988

World AIDS Day is marked for the first time. The day is an opportunity to show international solidarity in the fight against HIV and AIDS.

The decision to designate December 1 as World AIDS Day was made by the World Health Organization on January 28, 1988, at the World Summit of Ministers of Health held in London.

December 1 is also the last day of Canadian HIV/AIDS Awareness Week, which takes place every year from November 21 to December 1.

The Canadian AIDS Society and UNAIDS have the shared goal of putting an end to the AIDS epidemic by 2030.
