August 19, 1942

Allied forces launch the Raid on Dieppe, France, during the Second World War. Of the 4,963 Canadians who disembark, about 2,200 make it back to England. During the operation, more than 900 Canadian soldiers lose their lives, and nearly 2,000 are taken prisoner.

There will be disagreement about the need for and value of the raid on Dieppe. Some will describe it as needless carnage; others will claim that the operation proved necessary for the success of the D-Day invasion two years later, on June 6, 1944. Lessons learned at Dieppe probably helped to save thousands of lives on D-Day.
