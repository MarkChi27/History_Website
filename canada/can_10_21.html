October 21, 2014

Jordin Tootoo, the first Inuk to play in the National Hockey League (NHL), launches his book entitled All the Way: My Life on Ice.

In the book, co-written with Stephen Brunt, Tootoo recounts his story and the challenges he had to overcome throughout his career.

In June 2017, the Honourable David Johnston, Governor General of Canada, will present Jordin Tootoo with the Meritorious Service Medal – Civil Division, in recognition of his outstanding Indigenous leadership. He is recognized across the country for his efforts to promote healthy lifestyle choices in Canada’s North, as well as his Team Tootoo Fund foundation.

Jordin Tootoo, from Churchill, Manitoba, played his first NHL game for the Nashville Predators in 2003-2004. After eight seasons in Tennessee, he was traded to the Detroit Red Wings. He will later play for the New Jersey Devils and the Chicago Blackhawks.
