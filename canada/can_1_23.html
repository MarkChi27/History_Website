January 23, 1922

Canadian scientist Frederick Grant Banting and his research team test insulin on a human for the first time, treating a young patient at the Toronto General Hospital who is near death from diabetes. The patient recovers, proof of the importance of the discovery of insulin.

In 1923, Frederick Grant Banting and his colleague J.J.R. Macleod will jointly receive the Nobel Prize in Medicine for their discovery of insulin to treat diabetes.
