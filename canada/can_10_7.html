October 7, 1999

Adrienne Clarkson is the second woman to be appointed Governor General of Canada. Queen Elizabeth II, Canada’s Head of State, appoints her on the recommendation of Prime Minister Jean Chrétien.

Clarkson will be the first Canadian of Chinese origin to become the Crown’s representative. She had been an outstanding television host, journalist and writer.

Her appointment marks several firsts, including the absence of a military or political background.
