November 20, 1965

The Vanier Cup, the trophy for the winners of the Canadian university football championship, is won for the first time. The University of Toronto Varsity Blues triumph over the University of Alberta Golden Bears.

In spring 1966, Governor General Georges P. Vanier will present the first Vanier Cup in person to the Toronto players, at an official banquet at Varsity Stadium.

Referred to at the time as the Canadian College Bowl, the Canadian university football championship will officially become known as the Vanier Cup, the same name as the trophy, in 1982.
