October 17, 1967

The rock musical Hair premieres off-Broadway at the Public Theater in New York.

This spectacular production, with a score composed by Canadian pianist Galt MacDermot, will move to Broadway’s Biltmore Theatre in April 1968. The musical will play on Broadway for four years in a row, a total of 1,750 shows. Hair will be performed in nearly every country in the world, adapted to the big screen, and produce four Billboard hits: “Good Morning Starshine” (Oliver), “Easy to Be Hard” (Three Dog Night), “Aquarius” (The Fifth Dimension) and “Hair” (The Cowsills).
