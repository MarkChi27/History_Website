October 18, 1929

London recognizes that women have legal status as “persons,” thereby paving the way for the participation of Canadian women in public life and politics.

The difficult legal battle begun in 1927 by five Alberta women activists, later known as the “Famous Five,” is a decisive turning point in the struggle for women’s equality in Canada.

Lord John Sankey’s decision in the “Persons” case on October 18, 1929: women are persons under the law.

Since that historic judgment, the Governor General’s Awards in Commemoration of the Persons Case have been handed out every year in October.
