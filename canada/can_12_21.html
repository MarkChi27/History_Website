December 21, 1894

Senator Mackenzie Bowell is appointed Prime Minister of Canada to replace Sir John S.D. Thompson, who passed away in office.

Bowell was born on December 27, 1823, in Rickinghall, England. He immigrated to Canada with his parents in 1832. He was a founding member of the Canadian Press Association and its president from 1859 to 1866. An Orangeman, he began his political career in 1874 and served as minister in the governments of Sir John A. Macdonald and John Joseph Caldwell Abbott. In 1892, Bowell was named a Senator and became Leader of the Government in the Senate.

Bowell will serve as Prime Minister until 1896.
