July 27, 1996

Donovan Bailey wins the gold medal in the 100-metre dash at the Atlanta Olympic Summer Games, setting a new world record of 9.84 seconds. He will also win gold in the 4x100-metre relay with teammates Bruny Surin, Glenroy Gilbert and Robert Esmie.

Jamaican-born Bailey went to high school in Ontario and was a successful businessman who returned to sport after taking time to build his career.

Bailey is Canada's second gold-medal winner in the men's 100-metre race. Percy Williams won the first gold in 1928.
