April 26, 1900

The Hull Fire starts in a chimney. Half of the town of Hull, Quebec, and whole neighbourhoods in Ottawa, Ontario, burn to the ground.

The fire destroys the E.B. Eddy Match Co. and spreads to the woodlots of the Hull Lumber Company. The fire crosses the Ottawa River via the Chaudière Bridge and reduces 3000 homes and businesses to ashes.

The fire is stopped when water is released at the Hog’s Back dam.
