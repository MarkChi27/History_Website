August 16, 1896

Near present-day Dawson City, Yukon, Keish (alias Skookum Jim Mason) of the Tagish First Nation strikes gold in Rabbit Creek. After Keishâ€™s discovery, this Klondike River tributary will quickly be renamed Bonanza Creek and eventually become famous around the world.

The gold strike will spark the Klondike Gold Rush. In July 1897, the rest of the world will hear about the discovery of gold, and an unprecedented gold rush will start. Tens of thousands of prospectors will leave their homes in hopes of striking it rich. They will flock to Dawson City and the Klondike.

In 1994, the Government of Canada will designate Skookum Jim Mason as a National Historic Person for his role in this key moment in Canadian history.
