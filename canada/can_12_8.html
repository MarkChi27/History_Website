December 8, 1869

The Red River Métis form a first provisional government. They also draft the Declaration of the People of Rupert’s Land and the North-West. Issued by John Bruce and Louis Riel, the declaration proposes a negotiated settlement between Canada and the new provisional government about the governance of the North-West.

Louis Riel will begin negotiations with the Government of Canada, which will lead to the creation of the province of Manitoba in 1870.
