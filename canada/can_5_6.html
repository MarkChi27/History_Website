May 6, 1973

Sir Ernest MacMillan, Canada’s “musical knight,” passes away in Toronto, Ontario. Born in 1893 in Mimico, Ontario, MacMillan studied music in Toronto before heading to Paris in 1914 to study piano.

MacMillan was in Germany for the Bayreuth Music Festival when the First World War started. As a result, he spent the war in a German prison camp as a civilian prisoner.

In 1935, King George V knighted MacMillan on the recommendationof Prime Minister R.B. Bennett.
