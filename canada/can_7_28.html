July 28, 1914

On the eve of war, the Toronto Stock Exchange (TSX) closes, as does the New York Stock Exchange. A week later, the British Empire, including Canada, will enter the First World War.

Amid fears of economic panic, the TSX will remain closed for three months to protect investors.

The TSX will close for an entire trading day only once since then, on December 17, 2008, when a technical glitch will silence the trading floor.
