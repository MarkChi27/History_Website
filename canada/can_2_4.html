February 4, 1965

Hamilton Falls in Labrador becomes Churchill Falls. The Premier of Newfoundland, Joey Smallwood, renames the falls to pay tribute to British Prime Minister Sir Winston Churchill, who had died in London on January 24, 1965, at the age of 90. An inspiration to many Canadians during the Second World War, the legendary British prime minister will have several Canadian locations named after him, including a town, two rivers and the falls
