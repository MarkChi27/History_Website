May 7, 1920

The Group of Seven’s first exhibition opens at the Art Gallery of Ontario in Toronto. The show includes works by Franklin Carmichael, Lawren S. Harris, A.Y. Jackson, Franz Johnston, Arthur Lismer, J.E.H. MacDonald and F.H. Varley.

The exhibition will not be a great success, with only three canvases sold. The painters’ landscape art, although not a hit in 1920, will soon be coveted by collectors, galleries and museums around the world. In 2016, “Mountain Forms” by Lawren Harris will sell for a record-setting $11.4 million at auction.
