December 19, 1975

Bertha Wilson is the first woman appointed to the Ontario Court of Appeal.

Bertha Wilson was born in Kirkcaldy, Scotland, in 1923. She immigrated to Canada with her husband in 1949. After studying law at Dalhousie University, she was called to the bar in Nova Scotia. Wilson spent 16 years at a prestigious law firm in Toronto before she was named to the provincial supreme court.

On March 4, 1982, she will set a similar precedent, as the first woman appointed to the Supreme Court of Canada. She will serve as a Supreme Court justice until 1991.

Bertha Wilson will die in 2007 at the age of 83.
