May 19, 1986

Denys Arcand takes home the international critics’ FIPRESCI Prize at the Cannes Film Festival for Le Déclin de l’empire américain (The Decline of the American Empire). The film will win several Genie awards and be nominated for the Academy Award for Best Foreign Language Film, a first for a Canadian film. It will become the biggest-grossing movie in Quebec, bringing in $30 million. The Toronto International Film Festival will include the film twice in its list of the Top 10 Canadian Films of All Time.

Arcand will make two sequels to the film, The Barbarian Invasions (2003) and Days of Darkness (2007).
