August 14, 1943

The first Quebec Conference is under way at the Château Frontenac in the city of Québec, with Canada as host.

British Prime Minister Winston Churchill and United States President Franklin D. Roosevelt will hold discussions to secure an agreement about the upcoming landing of Allied forces in Europe and the end of the Second World War.

The host of the meeting is Canadian Prime Minister William Lyon Mackenzie King. He will remain in Québec throughout the discussions, which will end on August 24.

Churchill and Roosevelt will confer again at the second Quebec Conference in September 1944.
