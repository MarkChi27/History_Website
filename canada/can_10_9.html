October 9, 1867

In the city of Québec, 164 physicians hold a historic meeting to establish the Canadian Medical Association (CMA), founded to empower and care for patients. Charles Tupper will be elected the CMA’s first president.

The CMA will be an important player in key areas related to health, particularly the battle for medicare. The CMA will also produce several scientific publications, including the Canadian Medical Association Journal, and guides and magazines.

Today, the CMA has more than 85,000 members across Canada and continues to promote a vibrant medical profession and a healthy population.
